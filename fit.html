My Mindset (Young, Hungry & Talented)

Your post said you're looking for someone "young, hungry, and talented." I believe that's not just a set of skills, but a mindset. Here are a few stories that show you *why* I'm the 1 person for the job.

Proof I'm Young, Hungry, and Talented

Here is how my experiences show this mindset in action.

Hungry & Talented (My Drive)

TL;DR: From martial arts championships to turning a 'C' into a TA position, I thrive on hard challenges and have a deep-seated need to be great.


I’ve always been driven by the enjoyment of a hard challenge and the desire to be at my best when my best is needed.


This started early for me. In martial arts, I dedicated myself from age five to become a champion at local, state, national, and international levels. Later, when I took up basketball as a total beginner, I embraced the challenge by being the first one in and the last one out of the gym, channeling my energy into becoming the best player I could be. From being a bench warmer my freshman year to playing in the starting five in my junior year, I made huge strides in my game simply because I had an absolute need to be great.


This drive extends directly to my academics, too. I graduated from USC a full year early with a 3.9 GPA. A great example of my academic drive was in computer science: my Intro to Java course was really hard for me and was the first time I'd ever gotten a C on an exam. But, instead of giving up, I saw this as a challenge to conquer. I doubled down on my efforts, and just one semester later, I became a TA for that very same course.


Even this application is an example of my drive. I chose to build this multi-part website, sent in my "2-minute email," and created spec work to give it my all. I wanted to do everything I could to stand out and show you my commitment and drive firsthand - to demonstrate my hunger.

Hungry to Learn (Coachability)

TL;DR: I actively seek out direct, honest feedback because I know it's the fastest path to growth. I'm highly coachable and ready to be a sponge.


I was taught to always communicate what needs to be communicated, regardless of how difficult it may seem. This value has been a core part of my development.


A formative experience was my time in martial arts. As a competitor, after every single one of my tournaments, I would ask each of my and opposing schools’ senseis (teachers) for direct feedback on how I could improve. Hearing their critiques was difficult in the moment, but I learned to listen to those hard truths. This practice significantly accelerated my learning curve and was a key factor in winning numerous championships.


I also learned how to give candid feedback at a young age. I was advanced enough that my martial arts instructor would often ask me to help teach the adult classes (even as an eight year old kid). The adults took longer to learn the techniques, so I had to find ways to express the hard truth that they weren't getting it and show them how to improve, all while keeping them motivated and respected.


I'm eager to learn and ready to absorb all the feedback you can give. I know that's how I'll learn "4 years' worth in 1."

Talented & Reliable (My Character)

TL;DR: I'm guided by a strong moral compass, always keeping my word and doing what's right, even when it's difficult. My actions are driven by integrity.


I'm thoroughly motivated by doing what I judge to be the right thing, even when doing so is hard. I do what I say I’m going to do and I keep my word. This principle has guided me for as long as I can remember.


When I was 10, my parents trusted me to write client emails for deals worth over six figures because they knew they could rely on my judgment and integrity. People know they can rely on me and that I always strive to do the right thing, which is why I believe they gravitate toward working with me.


One example of this is my decision to go to law school. That choice was a direct result of seeing the injustice of a wrongful lawsuit against my family's business. My passion was always business, but I knew that learning the law was the right thing to do to protect honest, hardworking people. It was the harder path, but it was the right one. This is the same principle I bring to everything I do.


Another example, going years back, makes me smile every time I think about it. My friends at school hosted a competition to decide who is my best friend (we were young, doing what young children do haha). Though more than a decade has passed since this competition, the premise still stands: people respect and appreciate who I am and what I do so much that they are proud to be associated with me.

My Best Qualities

These are the personal attributes I bring to every team and every challenge.

Ability to Listen

I listen to truly understand, not just to respond. This helps me learn quickly, collaborate effectively, and make my colleagues feel heard and valued.

Energy

I bring a contagious, positive energy to every room I enter. People feel my kindness and positivity—I’ve even had strangers compliment my good energy. I genuinely enjoy raising the collective morale and ceiling of the group.

Quick & Eager Learner

Learning how to learn quickly is one of the highest ROI skills I've developed. Paired with a genuine eagerness, I inhale information, which will help me contribute to the team immediately.

Detail Oriented

My experiences in law and computer programming have trained me to be meticulous. I understand that every detail matters, a lesson reinforced by a childhood deal with my mom—$10 for every silly math mistake. I never made one again.

Hard Worker

I am one of the hardest workers I know and am eager to do whatever it takes to help the team win. I also reflect on my progress daily to do more of what's working, ensuring I work smart, not just hard.

Team Player

I thrive on working with awesome people and focus on the intangibles that make a team both fun and productive. I work to get buy-in on the mission, facilitate clear communication, and keep morale high.

Professional

I've spent thousands of hours interacting with high-profile clients and understand how to conduct myself with a high degree of professionalism, clarity, and precision.

Good Communicator

I listen well, write clearly, and read the room. I do my best to be articulate and easy to understand, and I make no assumptions—I spell things out to make sure everyone is on the same page.

Take Initiative & Responsibility

I take it upon myself to think ahead and act accordingly. I'm generally the first to offer help or execute on a new idea. If things go south, I'll be the first to take responsibility and find solutions.

Manage Priorities Well

Growing up juggling work, competitive sports, and academics taught me how to balance many tasks and work ahead of schedule. In challenging situations, I'm the one who stays calm and de-escalates.

A quick final note: I’m generally quite humble, but I've been super straightforward with my strengths in this application. We don’t have the luxury of getting to know each other over the course of days or weeks, so I wanted to be as clear as possible about the value I can bring to the team.
